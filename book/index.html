Mathematical Imaging

Online book for MATH 435

This online book is the narrative companion to the MATH 435 slides and notebooks. The slides are designed for class structure; the book is designed for slower reading, review, and project preparation.

The central identity of the course is developed in the motivation chapter: mathematical imaging begins with inverse problems and naturally leads to neural networks as learned inverse maps, learned priors, learned denoisers, or learned components of reconstruction algorithms.

The book grows from the stabilized course materials. Each chapter connects:

  • the mathematical model;
  • the computational idea;
  • the relevant figures;
  • links to the slide deck;
  • links to the runnable notebook.

How To Use This Book

Read each chapter before or after the corresponding class. Use the slides for the lecture structure and the notebook for experiments.

For each chapter, students should be able to answer:

  1. What is the mathematical object?
  2. What is the forward model?
  3. What information is lost or degraded?
  4. What computation tests the idea?
  5. What can go wrong?

Notation

We will use:

  • u for a continuous image function;
  • U for a discrete image array;
  • x=\operatorname{vec}(U) for a vectorized image;
  • A for a forward operator;
  • y for measured data;
  • \eta for noise;
  • \hat{x} for a reconstruction.

The meaning of A changes from chapter to chapter: it may be a mask, a blur, a Fourier sampling operator, or a more general measurement model.
