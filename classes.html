Weekly Roadmap

This page is the weekly entry point for the course materials. The course normally meets twice per week, so each row below is a week/module, not a single 75-minute meeting.

Each week has one mathematical story:

  • one book chapter for slower reading;
  • one slide deck for the two live sessions;
  • one notebook for computational experiments;
  • one practice thread connecting measurement, prior, computation, and evidence.

Holidays and official announcements may shift the exact calendar dates. The syllabus remains the authority for grading, assessment dates, and course policies.

Quiz 1 is planned for the final 20 minutes of the second meeting in Week 4. Quiz 2 is planned for the final 20 minutes of the second meeting in Week 12. See the quiz information page for student-facing logistics and preparation guidance.

Weekly Modules

Week Topic Session 1 Session 2 Materials
1 Images as mathematical objects Images as functions, arrays, and vectors. Forward models, masking, missing information, and first inverse problems. slides, book, notebook, Colab
2 Convolution and blur Kernels, point spread functions, and convolution as a forward model. Boundary conditions, matrix view, and why deblurring is unstable. slides, book, notebook, Colab
3 Fourier transform in imaging Fourier as projection, energy, Pythagoras, and global support. 2D spectra, convolution theorem, filtering, and stabilized deblurring. slides, book, notebook, Colab
4 Noise models and likelihoods Gaussian and Poisson noise as measurement models. Likelihoods as data terms, then Quiz 1 in the final 20 minutes. slides, book, notebook, Colab
5 Ill-posed inverse problems Well-posedness, non-uniqueness, missing data, and stability. Singular values, noise amplification, condition numbers, and stabilization. slides, book, notebook, Colab
6 Tikhonov regularization Regularized least squares and normal equations. SVD interpretation, choosing lambda, bias-variance, and project proposal. slides, book, notebook, Colab
7 Variational formulation Energies as models, convexity, and Euler-Lagrange equations. Gradient descent intuition, quadratic denoising, and model limitations. slides, book, notebook, Colab
8 Total Variation regularization TV, sparse gradients, edge preservation, and one-dimensional examples. Image denoising, staircasing, modeling limits, and practice synthesis. slides, book, notebook, Colab
9 Optimization methods Convexity, smooth optimization, gradient descent, and step size. Nonsmooth terms, proximal operators, ISTA, and convergence evidence. slides, book, notebook, Colab
10 Sparse reconstruction Sparsity, representation choice, and l1 versus l2 regularization. Underdetermined recovery, compressed sensing intuition, and sparse images. slides, book, notebook, Colab
11 Wavelets and multiscale representation Fourier global support, wavelet localization, Haar intuition, and multiscale energy. 2D subbands, thresholding, compression, denoising, and wavelet limits. slides, book, notebook, Colab
12 Model-based vs data-driven imaging Model-based imaging, data-driven imaging, and neural networks as operators. Learned priors, distribution shift, evaluation, then Quiz 2 in the final 20 minutes. slides, book, notebook, Colab
13 Plug-and-play and learned regularization Proximal algorithms, denoising interpretation, and the plug-and-play update. Learned regularization, deblurring, fixed points, convergence, and trust. slides, book, notebook, Colab
14 Stability, robustness, and ethics Stability tests, perturbation amplification, robustness axes, and project evidence. Hallucination risk, ethics, documentation, and project report preparation. slides, book, notebook, Colab
15 Project presentations and oral defenses Project presentations and discussion. Individual oral defenses and course synthesis. project page, syllabus

Weekly Workflow

Moment What To Do
Before Session 1 Read enough of the book chapter to recognize the main objects, formulas, and modeling question.
Session 1 Use the first part of the slide deck for concepts, derivations, and guided examples.
Between Sessions Revisit the main model and write one question about what the data support or what the model assumes.
Session 2 Use the second part of the slide deck for consequences, computation, notebook discussion, quizzes, or project work.
After Session 2 Run the notebook, change at least one important parameter, and write a short practice note.

Practice notes are study aids, not separate graded assignments. Course grading is governed by the syllabus.

Project Preparation Guide

The syllabus defines the assessed project moments as the proposal in Week 6, the report in Week 14, and the oral defense in Week 15. The other rows below are planning aids for the final project described on the project page. They do not create a separate grading category.

Week Milestone
6 Project proposal: team, tentative topic, data source, baseline, second method, first forward model, and reliability question.
7 Planning feedback: refine the model statement, data access, and implementation choices.
8 Baseline preparation: create degraded data and run a simple reconstruction.
10 Method development: implement two methods on the same test cases.
12 Evidence development: add a parameter sweep, quantitative metric, and first robustness or failure test.
14 Project report: final notebook, written report, claim-evidence table, LLM-use statement, and oral-defense preparation.
15 Final submission and individual oral defense.
