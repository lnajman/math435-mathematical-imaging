Planning Model

The course should be maintained as a weekly course, not as a list of isolated class meetings:

  • one weekly topic;
  • two 75-minute meetings;
  • one weekly slide deck;
  • one weekly notebook;
  • one book chapter;
  • one practice thread.

The notebook is part of the weekly activity load. When judging whether a deck is paced correctly, count notebook and code-reference slides as part of the session plan, even though they are not pure lecture slides.

Pacing Targets

Week Type Target Total Slides Notes
Normal two-session week 38-48 slides This includes title, plan, session dividers, activities, notebook/code slides, checkpoints, and next-week slides.
Quiz week 34-40 slides The final 20 minutes of Session 2 are reserved for the quiz, so the teaching portion should be leaner.
Heavy derivation week 32-42 slides Leave time for board work, student questions, and pauses in the derivation.
Project-focused week 30-40 slides Leave time for project discussion, model checks, and oral-defense preparation.

If a normal weekly deck has more than about 48 slides, first mark slides as optional or backup rather than deleting useful material. The instructor-facing decision is: what must be covered live, what can become a notebook prompt, and what can remain as review material.
